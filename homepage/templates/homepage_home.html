About Me

Occupation

I am currently a graduate student at Harvard Medical School studying cancer genetics using computational biology. Under the guidance of Professor Kevin Haigis and Professor Peter Park, I seek to improve our understanding of the role of KRAS, the most commonly mutated oncogene in cancer. Specifically, I am curious about the various, though limited, mutations commonly found in patients. Additionally, I am trying to explain the reason KRAS-driven cancers only arise in certain organs.
